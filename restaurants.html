What we know about restaurant operations that shapes how we build.

No-Shows

A no-show problem is a communication problem.

Most no-shows happen not because guests are inconsiderate but because nothing reminded them with enough urgency at the right time. A confirmation at booking, a reminder 24 hours out, and a same-day touchpoint reduce no-shows significantly. The restaurants not doing all three are leaving covers on the table.

Catering

Catering is the highest-margin revenue most operators underinvest in.

A catering or private event inquiry is worth 10-50x a standard cover. Response speed is the primary conversion driver — not price, not menu. The group that responds to an event inquiry within 10 minutes wins the booking at a rate that makes speed the competitive advantage, not quality alone.

Guest Retention

The guest relationship doesn't have to end at the door.

Restaurant operators invest heavily in the in-venue experience and almost nothing in the post-visit relationship. A guest who had a great dinner and received a thoughtful follow-up with a review request and a reason to return visits more frequently than one who didn't. The margin impact compounds across a multi-location group.
