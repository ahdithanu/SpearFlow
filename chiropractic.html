Three things we know about chiropractic operations that change how we build.

Retention

The 90-day cliff is the real problem.

Patients who finish a care plan and don't re-book within 90 days have a 67% chance of never returning to that practice. The window to intervene is narrow. An automated re-engagement sequence that runs at 30, 60, and 90 days closes it consistently. Most practices have none.

Operations

Staff turnover shouldn't be a crisis.

When one front desk coordinator holds all the operational knowledge, every departure breaks something. A documented, automated system means new staff have a framework on day one. The practice doesn't depend on a person to function.

Revenue

A no-show rate is a diagnostic, not a constant.

High no-show rates are symptoms: scheduling friction, weak confirmation sequences, or low patient trust. We audit for the root cause before building the fix. Technology applied to the wrong problem doesn't help.
