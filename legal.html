What we know about legal practice operations that shapes how we build.

Intake

The intake call is the case.

Most prospective legal clients call two or three firms. The one that responds first with a clear, professional experience wins the engagement. A firm that calls back the next morning is competing against one that answered at 9pm. First response wins in legal intake the same way it does in real estate.

Billable Time

Admin work is the most expensive work attorneys do.

When a $400/hour attorney spends time on scheduling, follow-up emails, and status updates, those are $400/hour tasks by cost but zero by billing. Automating the administrative layer doesn't just save time. It recovers the highest-cost hours in the practice.

Client Trust

Operational professionalism signals legal professionalism.

Clients hire attorneys they trust. Trust starts forming before the first consultation. A firm that responds quickly, confirms appointments, and communicates proactively about document needs signals the same attention to detail clients expect in their matter. The administrative experience is part of the legal experience.
